The networking paradox that's holding back your career 🎯

Most professionals approach networking backwards. They network when they need something, not when they can give something.

I've spent the last decade studying high-performers across industries, and here's the counterintuitive truth about networking:

The best networkers are the best givers.

They understand that relationships are investments with compound returns. When you consistently add value to others' lives, opportunities find you.

Three principles that transformed my approach:

1️⃣ Lead with curiosity - Ask about their challenges, not their job title
2️⃣ Be a connector - Introduce people who should know each other
3️⃣ Share knowledge freely - Your insights are more valuable than you think

The professionals in my network who've achieved extraordinary success all share this mindset: they build relationships as an end in itself, not as a means to an end.
